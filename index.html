  • 1828

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Previously in an area populated by the Cherokees, Macon County was formed in 1828 in honor of Senator and Speaker of the House, Nathaniel Macon. The cities included were Haywood, Franklin, Highlands, Norton, Rainbow Springs, Nantahala, Ellijay, Cowee, and Scaly Mountain.

  • 1829

    A Community is Emerging

    The official county government is formed! Macon County government is fully functional in its early stages. The first county courthouse is built in Franklin, NC.

  • 1861-1865

    Development Continues

    Throughout the American Civil War, the county faced some struggles. Though no battles occured, the population felt the weight of war. After it's end, the commnuity began to rally and seek a better place for themselves.

  • 1946-Present

    Expanding the Vision

    The Macon County Historical Society was formed to ensure the preservation of the Cherokee Nikwasi Mound. This was an important and symbolic move to recognize the Cherokee who once used the mound for traditional ceremonies.
